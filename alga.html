About

It’s estimated that by 2030, 16 of the 22 Pacific Island countries will no longer be able to meet their current fish consumption. The oceans are a key resource for the Pacific Islands, and they rely heavily on fishing. Seaweed is a major untapped market, and there are estimates that by 2030, seaweed markets will have a growth potential of up to $11.8 billion.

This is where Alga comes in. Alga plans to help the Pacific Islands utilise seaweed farming by providing an education programme and resources to help the Pacific Islands break into the industry. This will help to regenerate local fish populations while providing a new industry for the Pacific Islands to tap into.

01 Research

Starting with research, initially we looked through different online resources to find out what the key insights were around aquaculture.

With our initial research, we were able to go forward and define a problem and solution to start developing upon.

Personas were then able to be defined and developed to help empathise with the different target audiences' I also developed a user journey map for the primary persona, Oemwa, whose family lives in the Pacific.

02 Branding

Branding was developed by our team's graphic designer. Muted seaweed colours were used to provide a natural feel while also being presented as professional and educational.

I also created some minimal hand-drawn illustrations for our website content to provide a visual walkthrough of how Alga works.

03 UX/UI

Being the UX/UI designer, I worked solo on the website. With the lo-fi and mid-fi prototypes, I worked on making the website accessible for investors as well as regular people interested in donating to support the cause. What I soon discovered through testing and WIP reviews was that the website needed to better represent the brand identity and be more interactive and immersive, presenting itself as a respectable organisation.

With the final prototype, I aimed to create more of a storytelling experience, educating visitors about who Alga is, the process, how Alga will benefit the Pacific, and how the visitor can help support the cause.

04 Campaign

The graphic designer created posters and social media ads as collateral to target average Kiwis and investors, raising awareness and gaining support for Alga.

Finally, we created a long-term campaign timeline, as Alga is set in the speculative future.
