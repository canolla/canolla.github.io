Soaring With Jean

Collaborative Project

UX/UI

Installation Design

About

MOTAT (Museum of Transport and Technology), based in Auckland, wanted to commemorate their 60-year anniversary by opening their new science and technology center. My group and I were tasked with crafting a new interactive installation centred around the journey of Jean Batten.

Jean Batten was one of New Zealand's greatest aviators, achieving the first solo flight from England to New Zealand in 1936. The target audience we were tasked with designing for was families with children aged 5 to 12 years old.

01 Research

Initially, we started diving into some case studies from different digital and physical installations targeted at kids. We also looked into some insights obtained from various marketing agencies. Here are some of the initial insights we found.

With our initial insights on our target audience and research into the subject, we were able to define a problem and research question to investigate.

Trent, a member of the group, developed some personas and user journeys to help us better navigate the project and begin developing the solution.

After ideations, iterations and WIP meetings we finally reached a solution which we were happy to explore.

02 Branding

As a group, we developed our visual direction with the intention of creating an identity that captures the vintage feel of the 1930s while being bright and colourful enough to engage kids. The rubber-hose art style was a big inspiration for us throughout this project.

Sevarn, our lead visual designer, developed a Jean Batten character that would lead users through the Soaring with Jean experience. She's portrayed as an exciting and adventurous character who also serves as a way in which we can educate the children on Jean Batten's journey.

03 UX/UI

Beginning the UX/UI process of designing the Design-a-Plane experience, I delved into a few iterations and userflows to see what would be best for the target users to interact with. I decided that to be most effective, it was important to make the interface as simple and intuitive as possible. With the final iteration, I also took the design style from a flat display to a more rubber-hose inspired design.

With the final prototype complete, I prepared the elements for animating in After Effects. This allowed me to create this walkthrough-style animation of the interface, providing a look into how it would look to interact with.

04 3D Space

Trent created a 3D space using Blender to help better convey the layout of the space and how everything would look. He rendered the Design-a-Plane interfaces I designed and put them in the 3D space, showing what the physical appearance would look like.

05 Campaign

Finally, a campaign timeline was created to show how the new installation would be advertised and launched. The primary outlet where we would target our audience would be social media, which would be used to tease and show off the installation as we approach launch day.
