TrailRanger

Academic Research

UX/UI

Web Development

A project exploring how a digital solution could be made to encourage outdoor exploration

About

In recent years, young adults have become more and more disconnected from nature and increasingly connected to technology. This has negatively impacted their overall mental and physical health. Gamification is a concept that has been used a lot recently to make tasks like work and productivity more enjoyable and intuitive. With this project, the brief was focused on continual research and iterations.

From this, I was able to take the problem of isolation and disconnection from nature and target where their attention is (technology) through a mobile app to make outdoor exploration a gamified experience. This project serves as the groundwork for future research and development in hopes that it could one day become a solution for connecting young adults to the outdoors and with each other.

Research

Being an academic research project, research and experimentation was a majority of what was involved with this project. I begun gathering some academic resources giving me some insights into this topic, allowing me to identify the problem, and propose my research question.

From my research, important insights were gained into who the affected audience is, and how they are affected. Young adults are significantly more likely to spend less time outdoors than other ages, and outdoor environments are vital for adolescent wellbeing, serving as a potential social outlet, and providing connection with nature.

One of the key resources I discovered which supported this project was the Octalysis Framework. This is a framework crafted by gamification pioneer Yu-Kai Chou which analyses human motives through it's 8 core drives.

With the research I found, I was able to clearly identity the problem, who it affects, and the approach I can take to help aid this. This led me to the reseach question "How can a gamified mobile app be designed to help motivate and facilitate outdoor exploration among 18-24-year-olds?”.

UX/UI

After the initial research phase, I moved on to designing and developing the app. This process begun with defining the personas and their journeys, developing a sitemap, and then moving on to prototyping the app. This was an iterative process involving a number of tests and refinements with target users.

Final Artefact

Finally I developed the final prototype into an MVP using HTML/CSS/JS. This incorperated the culmination of research, testing and designs I had develeoped into a final, interactable product.

I also created a research poster featuring documentation of my journey from the beginning to end. Overall there's potential for further exploration and development in future, and I believe this project has potential to serve as a springboard for that.
