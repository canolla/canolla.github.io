In 2023, grocery prices were skyrocketing. This meant a lot more people who hadn't experienced food insecurity were having to reach out for support, but reaching out can be a lot harder than it sounds.

This inspired the creation of Nourish Network, an enterprise whose aim is to bring the community together to create a network of people including farmers who have surplus vegetables, eggs, milk and other produce to distribute for people to collect from.

The Brief

Create a new social and/or commercial enterprise relating to an identified present-day, real-world problem.

Research

Background Research

A problem which really stood out to me when looking into issues affecting Kiwis was food insecurity. Here are some of the insights I discovered when conducting background research.

  • 17% of 12-year-olds in New Zealand live in homes with moderate to severe food insecurity

  • At the beginning of 2023, fruit and vege prices in New Zealand increased by 22%

  • New Zealand's yearly food waste produces over 409,000 tonnes of carbon emissions. To offset this we would need to take over 150,000 cars off the road for one year or plant over 160,000 trees.

Empathy Research

I then conducted some empathy research to try to gain a better understanding of what New Zealanders were experiencing, and what their pain points were surrounding the topic.


3 Empathy Interviews were conducted with locals, and some pain points found were:

  • Not only the physical but also the mental toll that food insecurity can have is very taxing
  • Make the process of receiving food dignified, not something which is shameful. Positive not negative
  • People waste a lot of food when that food can still be used or turned into something else instead of going to landfill

Desk Research was also conducted, and three primary areas of weakness were discovered:

  • Economic

  • Environmental

  • Food Insecurity

Problem and Solution

Problem

How could we bring the community together to redistribute surplus food to families and younger generations, in the form of a positive experience?

Solution

Create a network of people including farmers who have surplus vegetables, eggs, milk and other produce to distribute for people to collect from

Target Audience

The target audience for Nourish Network doesn't have an age limit, but from research it was found that children often bare the brunt of food insecurity and have no control over their situations. So younger families and children were targeted in particular.

Two types of users were also determined, first 'the user' who benefits from the free produce, and second 'the distributor', who distributes their extra produce reducing food waste, and providing for those in need.

Visual Identity

The visual identity was developed via a thorough, iterative process which resulted in a logo featuring two letter 'N's representing the steam and leaves of a flower, symbolising the growth provided through this network. The process also delved into fun, colourful visuals shown in the stylescape, adding a playful element to the branding. Finally, an 11-page Brand Guidelines document was developed outlining how to use the brand elements.

Website Development

Looking at the website, the process to creating this started with gathering inspiration for layouts, creating sitemaps and wireframeing. I then went through the prototyping process while user testing and presenting the site along the way.

The final website is made to feel friendly and welcoming via the colours and friendly illustrations, and it's also simple enough so anyone could gain an understanding of the service just by scrolling through the homepage.

  • Lo-fi Prototype

    This was a basic prototype featuring the potential layout of the website, and through user-testing I found that it felt a bit cluttered at some points, having things like multiple nav arrows weren't necessary and users wanted more of a walkthrough of the website to understand what Nourish Network actually is.

  • Hi-Fi Prototype

    The Hi-fi prototype featured a lot more detail and was almost production ready. Through user testing and from feedback via a work in progress presentation I discovered that the scale of a lot of the text and elements was far too large and the chunky aesthetic was decreasing legibility and ease of access.

    Homepage & Donate tab

  • Become Distributor, Contact, & Find Distributor pages

  • Final Website

    For the final website, scale of text and elements were adjusted to be more suitable for viewing. There were clear call-to-actions put in the footer and the website was also made fully responsive and mobile friendly so it can be viewed on any device.

Campaign Timeline & Collateral

The campaign timeline and collateral were the final elements of the project to be developed.

First, distributor-targeted posters are deployed to gain some distributors who could share their surplus produce.

Then user-targeted posters along with social media ads are deployed advertising the free event targeting those who may need food support.

Finally, there's a free farmers market event in North Hagley Park featuring the newly joined distributors, as well as a Nourish Network booth which gives out informational pamphlets for people to take and learn more about the organisation.
