In 2023, grocery prices were skyrocketing. This meant a lot more people who hadn't experienced food insecurity were having to reach out for support, but reaching out can be a lot harder than it sounds.

This inspired the creation of Nourish Network, an enterprise whose aim is to bring the community together to create a network of people including farmers who have surplus vegetables, eggs, milk and other produce to distribute for people to collect from.

The Brief

Create a new social and/or commercial enterprise relating to an identified present-day, real-world problem.

Research

Background Research

A problem which really stood out to me when looking into issues affecting Kiwis was food insecurity. Here are some of the insights I discovered when conducting background research.

  • 17% of 12-year-olds in New Zealand live in homes with moderate to severe food insecurity

  • At the beginning of 2023, fruit and vege prices in New Zealand increased by 22%

  • New Zealand's yearly food waste produces over 409,000 tonnes of carbon emissions

Empathy Research

I then conducted some empathy research to try to gain a better understanding of what New Zealanders were experiencing, and what their pain points were surrounding the topic.

3 Empathy Interviews were conducted with locals, and some pain points found were:

  • - Not only the physical but also the mental toll that food insecurity can have is very taxing

  • - Make the process of receiving food dignified, not something which is shameful. Positive not negative

  • - People waste a lot of food when that food can still be used or turned into something else instead of going to landfill

Desk Research was also conducted, and three primary areas of weakness were discovered:

  • - Economic
  • - Environmental
  • - Food Insecurity

Problem

How could we bring the community together to redistribute surplus food to families and younger generations, in the form of a positive experience?

Solution

Create a network of people including farmers who have surplus vegetables, eggs, milk and other produce to distribute for people to collect from
