About

In 2023, New Zealand grocery prices were skyrocketing. This meant a lot more people who hadn't experienced food insecurity were having to reach out for support, but reaching out can be a lot harder than it sounds.

This inspired the creation of Nourish Network, an enterprise whose aim is to bring the community together to create a network of people, including farmers, who have surplus vegetables, eggs, milk, and other produce to distribute for people to collect from.

01 Research

Beginning with research, I looked into some stats and figures to get an idea of the painpoints Kiwi's are facing. Some of the key insights I found were that 17% of 12-year-olds in New Zealand live in homes with moderate to severe food insecurity.

Secondly, at the beginning of 2023, fruit and vege prices increased by 22%. Finally, New Zealand's yearly food waste produces over 409,000 metric tonnes of carbon emissions.

I then conducted empathy interviews with three local Kiwis to gain a better understanding of what their opinions were on food insecurity in New Zealand. 

From my initial research, I was able to define a problem and solution to continue with.

Then I developed two primary personas to further empathise and understand the target audiences and their painpoints. The first persona is Melissa, she's a single mother who has been struggling to put food on the table. The second persona is Corey, he's a farmer who supplies local businesses with produce, but he's often left with surplus produce which goes to landfill.

From the personas, I then created a user journey map for each of them to outline and gain a better understanding of the path that users may take when discovering and using Nourish Network. This helped me to pinpoint any potential holes in the solution which I may have looked over or not considered prior to mapping out their journeys.

02 Branding

The branding for this project was developed iteratively, with the goal of achieving a brand that feels professional but is also warm and friendly enough to be approachable for anyone to use without judgement. An 11-page brand guidelines document was also developed for this project.

Minimal, cut-out-style illustrations and lottie animations were created to help add to the welcoming, approachable feel. This style was also chosen to convey a natural, organic feeling, which helps to convey the idea of fresh produce.

03 UX/UI

The UX/UI process began with gathering layout inspiration, creating sitemaps, wireframing, and lofi prototyping. In this stage I was getting a general layout for the website and how different features would be laid out.

From what I had learned through user testing, it was essential to make browsing the site straight forward and as simple as possible for anyone to use the site freely.

So I developed the Hi-Fi prototype with a homepage which aims to explain who Nourish Network is, what it does, and then guide the user to the map where they can discover Nourish Network locations.

I also made the Become a Distributor page for those who wish to distribute produce, and a donate option for those who want to support the cause.

04 Web Development

After the prototyping process, with the user testing feedback I'd gained, I was able to move on to developing the website. I developed the website in VS Code using HTML/CSS/JS. I also connected Google Maps API to create a map of food distributors for users to browse through.

05 Collateral

To support the rollout and launch of this event, I created a campaign and collateral to gain awareness and get this project off the ground. First, distributor-targeted posters are deployed to gain distributors to distribute their produce.

Then user-targeted posters along with social media ads are deployed to raise awareness about the launch of Nourish Network, starting with a free farmers market.

Finally, pamphlets are designed for the farmers market to give to those who attend, letting them know about who Nourish Network is and how it works.
